ITEMS found in the maze have a virety of effects:

Each time you pick up a coin you'll gain XP which may cause you to level up, increasing your stats and healing you. At the end of a run, you'll convert your coins into winnings that can be used to pick new upgrades for each future run.
 You wouldn't be here if you didn't want to win money, so what are you waiting for? Pick these up!
Keys are used to open doors and treasure chests. Each lock you open consumes 1 key.
 You should probably save most of your keys for treasure chests, but don't be afraid to open a door if you need to.
Bombs are used to break walls in the eight spaces adjacent to you. Any monsters in those spaces will be slowed, allowing you to strike them first no matter what their speed score is.
Each time you pick up bombs you'll gain 4 of them. You can't hold very many, so don't be too stingy with them.
In addition to doing everything a regular bomb does, Power Bombs deal 20 damage to near-by monsters.
Once you find the Power Bombs, all of your bombs will be Power Bombs.
Using a potion heals you, maxes out your MP, and removes all debuffs.
Having a potion handy is the best way to stay alive.
In addition to doing everything a regular potion does, Improved Potions grant you 100 points of the Regain buff.
Once you find the Improved Potions, all of your potions will be Improved Potions.
Picking up a gem will grant 12 HP, 6 MP, 2 ATK, 2 Perception and 25 + 1.2X coins, where X is the current room number.
 Never leave a gem behind!

Each time you pick up a BOOK you'll either gain a new spell or bonus MP:

Picking up this book grants you 1 bonus MP.
 This is the least exciting book, but don't skip it. You can't find the higher level spells until you have a certain amount of bonus MP granted by books or the Stormcaller Upgrade!
The Bolt spell costs 12 MP and deals 2 damage per level.
 Bonus MP required: 0
The Bolt 2 spell gives monsters beaten by it a 25% chance to drop coins.
 Bonus MP required: 2
The Bolt 3 spell also inflicts the slow debuff on monsters it hits.
 Bonus MP required: 4
The Bolt 4 spell fires in all four basic directions.
 Bonus MP required: 10
The Bolt 5 spell also removes slip, stone, poison, and first-strike powers from monsters it hits.
 Bonus MP required: 12
The Blur spell costs 6 MP and gives your next attack the first-strike buff.
 Bonus MP required: 0
The Blur 2 spell also ignores any physical resistance the monster may have.
 Bonus MP required: 2
The Might spell costs 4 MP and makes your next attack deal 50% more damage.
 Bonus MP required: 0
The Might 2 spell also adds knock-back to your next attack.
 Bonus MP required: 2
The Ward spell costs 4 MP and gives you one point of physical damage reduction.
 Bonus MP required: 0
The Ward 2 spell give three points of physical damage reduction.
 Bonus MP required: 2
The Ward 3 spell give three points of magical and physical damage reduction.
 Bonus MP required: 14
The Heal spell costs 10 MP and heals you 8 HP.
 Bonus MP required: 0
The Flash spell costs 16 MP and lets you teleport. The farther away you try to teleport, the greater the chance the spell will fail.
 Bonus MP required: 0
The Flash 2 spell is half as likely to fail.
 Bonus MP required: 8

Equipped WEAPONS will add damage to each of your attacks equal to the enhancement bonus of the sword, as well as the additional special effects:

Storm Sword This sword does 2 extra bonus damage in addition to its enhancement bonus.
Lightning spells do an extra +1 damage per level.
There is a 1 in 8 chance each round that a lightning strike will attack your foes.
The lightning will deal 2X damage, where X is the enhancement bonus of the sword.
This is a good weapon to use early while the 2 bonus damage is still significant, or later with other items that improve your lightning abilities.
Brawling Blade Gain 5 HP.
Each time you enter a new room or pick up a coin gain 3 layers of damage protection.
 This is a good weapon to use early, before you pick up other powerful damage protection abilities.
Breaking Blade When you use a potion it will create a bomb blast.
Each time you make an attack there is a chance to create a bomb blast.
The chance on attack is equal to 5+X% were X is the enhancement bonus of the sword.
If you have Power Bombs, the blasts this weapon creates will be power bomb blasts instead. If you manage to find a Powder Belt or Dwarf Cape too, the explosion chance will stack.
Warp Sword When you teleport you'll recover from all debuffs except poison and heal slightly.
The healing is equal to 2X where X is the enhancement bonus of the sword.
If you don't have other debuff protection this can be a strong weapon.
Assassin Knife When you attacking a monster with full HP, deal a lot of extra damage.
The extra damage is equal to 4X, where X is the enhancement bonus of the knife.
Avoid using this weapon with items that cause lightning strikes.
Vital Blade Gain 12X HP, where X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the sword.
 More HP makes potions much better, and this weapon gives a lot of HP.
Staff of the Magi While wielding this weapon, you can cast all six basic spells even if you haven't found any books.
This weapon is powerful at the beginning of a run, but you should replace it quickly- it becomes worthless once you've picked up the sixth book.
Flux Blade This weapon's bonus will randomly change to a number from 2 to 13 each time you enter a new room.
In rooms sealed by battle locks the lock will decrease by 4 instead of 1 each time you beat a monster.
This weapon can be strong in the early rooms before you find a weapon with a high permanent enhancement bonus, but its real power comes into play in the more dangerous combat lock rooms.
Wind Sword Your attacks cause knock-back. A knocked back monster will be pushed, possibly breaking a
block. If you knock a monster back into another monster, the bumped monster will take bonus damage equal to your current ATK value without getting a chance to counter-attack.
This is one of the best swords, but can be replaced with a belt of strength or using the second level of the Might spell.
Sapping Sword Each time you hit a monster its ATK will be reduced by by X, where X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the sword. The Troll and Minotaur are immune to this effect.
This sword is perfect for fighting hearty monsters that take a lot of hits to beat.
Gambler's Blade This sword does 3 times more bonus damage than its enhancement bonus.
Gain 5X HP and 10X MP where X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the sword.
If you would die but have more than 1000 gold coins, you'll lose them and heal.
Extra monsters will appear.
This sword is strong enough to make up for the extra monsters, especially if you're using items that boost your Stealth. Before your unlock all of the upgrades you may want to consider avoiding it when have 1000 coins, as using the death protection means you won't get to spend those coins on upgrades.

Equipped HELMS will provide some kind of boost to your stats based off of it enhancement bonus, as well as the additional special effects:

Helm of Health Gain 10X HP, where X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the helm.
 A solid HP increase that you won't regret.
Helm of Power Gain 6X MP, where X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the helm.
 If you prefer to finish off your enemies with spells, you're going to need as much extra MP as you can find.
Helm of Sight Gain 4X Perception, where X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the helm.
You'll gain 2 bonus XP each time you find a hidden door, trap, or chest.
This helm is best early in the run, when the small XP bonus will be more noticeable. If you happen to find one with a particularly high bonus it might be worth keeping late into the run when traps become more dangerous.
Hero's Helm Gain 5X HP, 2X MP, and X Perception, where X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the helm.
Gain one bonus HP each time you enter a room. This bonus HP remains even if you change helms.
 This well rounded helm works with any build. Very good if found early.
Crystal Cap Grants 3X bonus damage, where X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the helm.
Gain 1 point of stealth.
Casting any Ward spell grants 1 XP.
You have 30% less max HP.
It can be risky to use a helm that reduces your HP, but the stats it grants are second to none. Consider using this with a Gambler's Blade for a very high ATK score.
Ribbon Grants immunity from the four debuffs: Thorns, Slippery, Poison, and Petrify.
 This helm makes fighting many of the monsters much simpler, because you won't have to think about their debuffs at all. Since it does not grat any kind of stat, not all builds will want to use this item.
Legend Helm Gain 5X HP, 5X MP, and 4X Perception, where X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the helm.
Lightning spells cost you 2 less MP to cast. 		Like all of the Legend items, this grants huge stat bonuses. Unlike the other Legend items, this one takes the same slot as the Ribbon. It might not be worth using if you don't have another way to deal with debuffs.

Equipped CAPES will provide some kind of boost to your stats based off of it enhancement bonus, as well as the additional special effects:

Cape of Health Gain 10X HP, where X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the cape.
 A solid HP increase that you won't regret.
Cape of Power Gain 6X MP, where X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the cape.
 If you prefer to finish off your enemies with spells, you're going to need as much extra MP as you can find.
Cloak of Stealth Gain X Stealth, where X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the cape.
The more Stealth you have the fewer monsters will show up. This can be very helpul in later rooms.
Cape of Humility The random hazards found as you move through the maze will be the difficulty of hazards 2X rooms earlier, where X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the cape.
 The normal formula for random hazards appearing is 1 chance per 10 rooms you've cleared, meaning by room 40 you're looking at up to 4 hazards. If you get one of these capes with a decently large bonus, you'll encounter a lot less hazards overall.

Golden Cloak Each room will have X extra treasures, where X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the cape.
The treasures could be coins, potions, bombs, keys, books, or equipment.
 This is a great item if you're confident you can survive without one of the other very strong cape effects that help you more directly.
Dwarf Cape Each floor you'll gain a bomb if you're not already at your max bombs.
Each attack has a 5% chance of creating a bomb blast.
 Never running out of bombs is incredibly helpful. The 5% chance of creating a bomb blast when you attack stacks with other similar effects.
Legend Cloak Gain 6X MP, where X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the helm.
Each room will have X extra treasures, where X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the cape.
Each floor you'll gain a bomb if you're not already at your max bombs.
 This is one of the stronger Legend items as it duplicates the Golden Cloak and most of the Dwarf Cape while also boosting your MP. The only drawback is opportunity cost- using it means you won't be able to max your Stealth stat or use the Humility ability.

Equipped ARMOR will provide some kind of boost to your stats based off of it enhancement bonus, as well as the additional special effects:

Armor of Health Gain 15X HP. Each time you attack gain X points of the Regen buff.
X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the armor.
 A solid HP increase that you won't regret.
War Armor Gain 5X HP, and 2 damage.
X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the armor.
The wearer is immune to Poison.
 The stats on this armor aren't very good, but poison immunity is nothing to ignore. Worth using until you find something better.
Power Suit Gain 10X HP, and X damage, where X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the armor.
The wearer is immune to Poison and Thorns.
 This high quality armor is perfect for melee focused builds.
Shifting Robe You can cast the Warp spell even if you haven't found book 6.
The Warp spell costs you 2 less MP.
 Early in the run this armor grants you a lot of mobility. Pairs nicly with a Warp Sword.
Swiftmail Gain 5X HP and heal X each time you use the Warp spell.
X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the armor.
You can cast the Warp spell even if you haven't found book 6.
The Warp spell costs you 2 less MP.
Gain 1 Speed.
 This high quality armor is perfect for evasive builds.
Robe of Eyes Gain 4X Perception, wehre X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the armor.
Grants immunity to the petrify debuff. Books will appear 50% more often. 		While it can be easy to collect the first six basic spell books, finding the more advanced ones takes a lot longer. Wear this armor if you want to get to the powerful spells quickly.
Robe of Storms Lightning spells cost you 2 less MP.
There is a 1 in 8 chance each round that a lightning strike will attack your foes.
The lightning will deal 2X damage, where X is the enhancement bonus of the armor.
While not a great armor on its own, if you stack several items that create lightning strikes you can deal a lot of damage to the monsters without ever getting near them.
Storm Plate Gain 5X HP and 5X MP.
Lightning spells cost you 2 less MP.
There is a 1 in 8 chance each round that a lightning strike will attack your foes.
The lightning will deal 2X damage, where X is the enhancement bonus of the armor.
This high quality armor is perfect for spell-caster builds.
Lucky Armor Gain 5X HP, where X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the armor.
You'll find 25% more gold coins. 		This armor is weak in combat, but 25% more coins adds up quickly.
Ghost Armor Gain 15X HP and 6MP, where X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the armor.
Gain 2 ATK and 1 Speed.
This armor's enhancement bonus improves whenever you pick up a Power Orb. 		This armor is a great pick for runs with the "Orbless" upgrade, but will be out shined by high bonus armors found late rooms in other runs.

Equipped BRACERS will provide some kind of boost to your stats based off of it enhancement bonus, as well as the additional special effects:

Bracers of Health Gain 10X HP. Your Heal spells cure poison.
X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the bracers.
 A solid HP increase that you won't regret.
Mage Brace Gain 6X MP.
There is a 1 in 8 chance each round that a lightning strike will attack your foes for 2X damage.
Each time you cast a Ward spell, gain 2 + 1.5X points of the Regain buff.
X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the bracers.
 This item can provide a lot of healing, and makes the Ward spell very cost effective.
Strike Bracer Gain 1 speed and 1 to 20 bonus damage.
The damage bonus will increase based on the current room number.
(I.e. 2 bonus damage in room 10 and 6 bonus damage in room 30.) 		Bonus attack damage is never a waste.
Assassian Bracer When you attacking a monster with full HP, deal a lot of extra damage.
The extra damage is equal to 3X, where X is the enhancement bonus of the bracers.
Avoid using this with items that cause lightning strikes.
Thief Glove You won't trigger traps that you have successfully spotted.
If your perception score is high enough, this item will protect you from a lot of damage.
Midas Fist Gain 1 bonus ATK damage.
Each monster you beat has a 5X% chance of dropping coins.
X is equal to the enhancement bonus on the bracers.
 Beating a bunch of monsters with this equipped is one of the fastest ways to level up. Downgrades from amazing to just good on runs with the "Daredevil" upgrade.

Equipped BELTS will provide some kind of boost to your stats based off of it enhancement bonus, as well as the additional special effects:

Belt of Health Gain 10X HP. Gain 4 bonus HP each time you drink a potion.
X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the belt.
 A solid HP increase that you won't regret. The bonus HP remains even after you change belts.
Belt of Strength Your attacks deal X extra damage.
Your attacks cause knock-back. A knocked back monster will be pushed, possibly breaking a block. If you knock a monster back into another monster, the bumped monster will take bonus damage equal to your current ATK value without getting a chance to counter-attack.
Your bombs and knock-backs can break trees.
 This is one of the best belts, but can be mostly replaced with a Wind Sword or using the second level of the Might spell. This is the only way to break trees.
Belt of Pockets Lets you carry 8 more Bombs and 4 more Potions.
 This is a nice effect, but there are better belts to use.
Monk's Cord Gain 1 Stealth.
Picking up a coin heals you for X, and grants your next attack the Power Attack buff.
X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the belt.
The main perk of this item is the Stealth, but a little healing is nice too.
Golden String Gain X MP each time you beat a monster, where X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the belt.
Being mana burned only takes half of your MP instead of all of it.
 This item lets you cast several more spells per room, and is an auto-include for any caster builds.
Powder Belt Each attack has a 4X% chance of creating a bomb blast.
 If you have Power Bombs, the blasts this weapon creates will be power bomb blasts instead. If you manage to find a Breaking Blade or Dwarf Cape too, the explosion chance will stack.
Legend Belt Gain 5X HP, 5X MP and Picking up coins heals you for X, where X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the belt.
You can carry 4 more bombs and 2 more potions.
 Like the other Legend items, you usually want to use this if you can find it. The only notable exception is that some players will favor the Belt of Strength.

Equipped BOOTS will provide some kind of boost to your stats based off of it enhancement bonus, as well as the additional special effects:

Boots of Health Gain 10X HP, where X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the boots.
 A solid HP increase that you won't regret.
Boots of Power Gain 6X MP, where X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the boots.
 If you prefer to finish off your enemies with spells, you're going to need as much extra MP as you can find.
Iron Boots Take no damage from spike traps.
 Spike traps are all common and deal more damage the farther you go into the maze. This common item lets you ignore them, but won't typically protect you more than
Frozen Boots Walk over water as if you have the Swimming upgrade.
Each time you step on water you'll heal 1 to 6 HP and recover 1 to 6 MP. 		Most of this item is replaced by a single upgrade, but its healing effect has some use if you want to fight a lot of water monsters.
Swamp Walkers Swamp tiles will not drain your XP / HP / MP. Losing your XP to a swamp tile feels bad, and this item protects you form that. Always a decent choice.
Boots of Stealth Gain X Stealth, where X is equal to the enhancement bonus of the boots. More Stealth means less monsters. Most of the time, these will be the best boots.
